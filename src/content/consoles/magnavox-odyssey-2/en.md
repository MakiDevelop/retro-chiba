**The first home console with a built-in physical keyboard** — a 49-key membrane unit that Magnavox used to position the system as "education plus games," even though mainstream titles rarely depended on it. Europe and Japan received the same hardware as the **Philips Videopac G7000**, where it actually outsold the North American Odyssey². The 1981 Atari lawsuit over *K.C. Munchkin!*'s resemblance to Pac-Man — which Atari won — became **the home-console industry's first successful game-design copyright case**. After Magnavox exited North America in 1984, Philips kept the line alive in Europe and Brazil with the upgraded **Videopac+ G7400** through 1986.
