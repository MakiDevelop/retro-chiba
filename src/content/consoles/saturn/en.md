Sega's fifth-generation flagship. Strong in Japan (5.8M units, healthy third-party support), the most credible domestic rival to the PlayStation. The international story was different: the 1995 surprise E3 launch (four months ahead of the announced North American date) alienated retail and press, the dual-CPU architecture was punishing for developers, and the system stalled at retail. The community-consensus 9.26M worldwide figure — surfaced by Twitter retro circles using Famitsu and industry data — is meaningfully more generous than the older "complete failure" narrative.
