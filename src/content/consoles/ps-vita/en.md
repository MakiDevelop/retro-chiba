The successor to the PSP — technically a multiple-times step up (**5-inch OLED, two analog sticks, a rear touchpad**), but **proprietary Memory Cards priced at $99 for 32 GB** and a smartphone-gaming market four years into the iPhone era squeezed the system from both sides. Sony quietly walked back first-party support; the platform survived to 2019 on indie support and Japanese niche genres — Persona, Danganronpa, Gravity Rush. **Sony's last handheld to date.** After Vita's discontinuation, Sony redirected the company's portable resources entirely into the PlayStation 4 and 5.
