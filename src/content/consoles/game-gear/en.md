**The first color-and-backlit handheld** — and technically ahead of the Game Boy on every axis (the Master System core ported to portable, 32 simultaneous colors against the Game Boy's four shades of gray). But **six AAs for three to five hours** — one-sixth of the Game Boy's endurance — turned the accessory of choice into a bag of spare batteries. Sega's "Bring Your TV With You" campaign pushed an external TV-tuner module that further muddled the platform's positioning. The 10.62 million units at 1997 discontinuation came in at roughly one-tenth of the Game Boy's lifetime — Sega's clearest defeat on Nintendo's home turf.
