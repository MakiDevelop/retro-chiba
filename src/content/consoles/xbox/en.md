Microsoft's first home console — the physical incarnation of Bill Gates's "PC into the living room" ambition. **The first console to ship with a built-in hard drive, an Ethernet port, and a unified online service** — Xbox Live, launched in 2002, effectively defined what "online console gaming" would mean. Japan was a commercial disaster (opening-day sales lower than SEGA's worst forecasts for the dying Dreamcast), but Halo carried the system to roughly GameCube-level share in North America and Europe. **Microsoft lost approximately $4 billion on the original Xbox**, treating the loss as the entry fee for the long-term hardware strategy that paid off with the Xbox 360.
