**Ex-Palm engineers betting on a "Palm OS handheld for games" category** — technically credible (dual analog sticks, 480×320 color, an ARM 200 MHz CPU, full Palm application compatibility). But the timing was awful: within months of the October 2003 launch, **Sony announced the PSP and Nintendo announced the DS, both shipping the very next year** — two 80M-and-154M-class machines that left no oxygen for a $299–399 niche handheld. **The Palm OS itself was simultaneously being eroded by the early signals of the iPhone era**, with the entire PDA ecosystem heading for collapse. Tapwave filed bankruptcy in 2005 — an early casualty of the early-21st-century convergence wave that absorbed PDAs and small handhelds into smartphones.
