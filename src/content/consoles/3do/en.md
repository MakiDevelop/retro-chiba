Founded by Electronic Arts founder Trip Hawkins, the 3DO Company licensed the hardware specification to Panasonic, GoldStar, Sanyo, and others — **a rare attempt at an open-hardware home console**. The 1993 North American launch at $699 placed it against the SNES and Genesis at three to four times their price; the market response was as expected. The 3DO Company exited hardware in 1996, pivoted to software publishing under Hawkins, and went bankrupt in 2003.
