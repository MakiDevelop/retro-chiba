**The first home video game console**, released September 1972 — three years ahead of Atari Pong. Ralph Baer developed it from his 1968 "Brown Box" prototype. No CPU; the system was pure analog circuitry. The screen showed white sprites on black, with **plastic color overlays the player taped onto the television** providing level art, and physical dice and play money included as props for some titles. After Pong launched in 1975, Magnavox sued Atari and settled in 1976 — a historic IP cross-license that set the precedent for every patent dispute in the home-console industry that followed.
