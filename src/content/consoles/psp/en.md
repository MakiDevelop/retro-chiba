Sony's first handheld and the only credible direct challenge to Nintendo's handheld dominance. **A 4.3-inch widescreen, UMD optical disc, and built-in Wi-Fi** made the PSP simultaneously a game machine, a personal media player, and a small networked device. PS2-class 3D graphics in a pocket-sized form factor, with a third-party catalog of unusual strength — Monster Hunter Portable created the "hunting party" restaurant phenomenon across Japan and East Asia. The 80M+ lifetime fell short of the DS's 154M, but it remains **the single moment in handheld history when Sony successfully dented Nintendo's hold on the category**.
