Nintendo's definitive answer to the spec war: **don't fight it**. The Wii deliberately skipped HD output, ran on a barely-uprated GameCube CPU, and shrank to a size that fit any TV cabinet. The Wii Remote — infrared pointer plus accelerometer — opened the platform to **people who had never played a video game**. Wii Sports as a pack-in turned mothers, grandparents, and physical-therapy clinics into Nintendo customers. **101.63 million units lifetime** makes the Wii the second-best-selling Nintendo home console of all time (between the NES at 61.91M and the Switch above it). It remains one of the cleanest "blue ocean" cases in tech industry history.
