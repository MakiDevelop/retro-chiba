**The first handheld that ran the full home-console cartridge library** — HuCards plugged into the TurboExpress and the PC Engine catalog ran natively. Technically extraordinary (the same CPU as the PC Engine console, color backlit display, optional TurboVision NTSC tuner add-on), but **$249.99 was 2.8× the Game Boy's $89.99 launch price** — the cutting-edge specs sat awkwardly in the mass-market handheld category Nintendo had defined a year earlier. NEC exited in 1994. **The TurboExpress was the first machine to articulate the "handheld = console" idea** — twenty-five years before the Switch made it commercially viable.
