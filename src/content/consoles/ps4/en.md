Sony's precision counter to Microsoft's stumble. The **"For the players"** campaign mapped directly onto the Xbox One's missteps: a forced Kinect, forced online check-in, and a $499 launch price. The PS4 came in $100 cheaper, with no mandatory accessory and no always-online — and cleared one million units on day one. The shift to AMD x86-64 (a console-industry first) collapsed cross-platform porting costs and produced the most complete third-party catalog of any generation. **117.5 million lifetime** makes the PS4 the second-best-selling home console ever, behind only the PS2.
