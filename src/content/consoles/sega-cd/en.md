A **CD-ROM expansion** for the Mega Drive, not a standalone console. Added a second 68000 CPU and a graphics ASIC, pushing the Mega Drive into the "multimedia gaming" era — but the timing was wrong, the software lagged, and the FMV-game vogue turned most of the catalog into clumsy interactive movies. Commercially a failure at ~2.7 million units. The first link in a chain of Sega add-ons (Sega CD → 32X → Saturn → Dreamcast) that would end Sega's hardware business by the early 2000s.
