**A single ASIC with Pong burned into it** — Atari's 1975 conversion of the 1972 arcade Pong into the home market. Distributed exclusively through Sears, it sold out that Christmas. The catalog was a single game (and Magnavox would point out that game closely resembled Odyssey's Table Tennis), but **this was the moment the home console industry first proved itself commercially viable**. Atari followed up with Super Pong, Ultra Pong, and a dozen related variants. The 1976 patent settlement with Magnavox formally licensed Atari into the legitimate home market and unblocked the rest of the decade.
