Sega's last home console. Technically a generation ahead — a built-in modem brought online play to the living room, the PowerVR2 graphics held up against the next-generation PS2, GD-ROM doubled CD capacity. But when the PS2 launched in 2000 with DVD playback and PlayStation 1 backward compatibility, Dreamcast sales collapsed. **Sega exited the hardware business permanently in 2001** — closing a 24-year arc as a home-console maker.
