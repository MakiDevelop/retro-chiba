Microsoft's third console, **the cautionary tale of the eighth generation**. The E3 2013 unveiling led with always-on Kinect, TV integration, **a mandatory 24-hour online check-in, and restrictions on used-game sharing** — every bullet of which alienated the core market. Microsoft reversed the worst policies within a week, but the perception had set; combined with a $100 price premium and the bundled Kinect, the PS4 took a roughly 2:1 lead in the first year that never closed. Phil Spencer's promotion to Xbox head in 2014 began the long course-correction — Game Pass subscriptions, deep backward compatibility, and the Bethesda/Activision acquisitions — but the Xbox One's lifetime sales remained **dramatically behind the PS4**, marking the inflection point of Microsoft's console fortunes.
