**The first handheld where fighting games were genuinely playable** — SNK condensed the arcade Neo Geo MVS's fighting heritage into a portable, with an **eight-way microswitch joystick** that put arcade-grade input on a handheld for the first time. **40,960 colors and 40 hours of battery life** put it technically ahead of the Game Boy Color. *SNK vs. Capcom: Match of the Millennium* (1999) was the first cross-publisher fighting-game crossover in history. But SNK was already in financial decline (the company would file bankruptcy in 2001), and Pokémon Yellow plus the Game Boy Color owned the same window. SNK exited the handheld business one year in. **Short-lived but exceptionally cult** — the X retro community still holds it up as the high-water mark for fighting games on a handheld.
