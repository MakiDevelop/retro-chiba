**The only home console ever shipped with a built-in display.** The 9-inch vector CRT was integrated into the chassis, removing the need for any external television. The graphics were **pure vector** — line-drawn, not raster sprites — giving the system the clarity of arcade *Asteroids* and *Battlezone* at a time when home machines could not approach those games on a standard TV. The screen was monochrome; **plastic color overlays attached to the screen** to fake color, an idea inherited from the Magnavox Odyssey. Production ended early in the 1983 crash, but the combination of technical singularity and small unit count (250,000) keeps the Vectrex a **collector's perennial in the retro community**.
