The last stand of Atari's second home-console era. Marketed as **"the world's first 64-bit console"** — technically a 64-bit graphics bus, with the CPU side a 32-bit Tom chip and a 16-bit 68000 helper. Developer-hostile to a degree that even Atari loyalists couldn't paper over; the third-party catalog stayed thin. The 1996 Atari/JTS merger ended hardware operations at roughly 250,000 units sold. It marks the close of Atari's 24-year arc as a home-console maker (1972–1996); from that point on, "Atari" survived as a brand and IP licensee passed through several owners.
