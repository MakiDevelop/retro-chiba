Apple's only serious attempt at the home-console market — and a half-hearted one. Apple licensed the architecture to Bandai as a kind of cut-price Mac OS derivative, with no first-party game roster, no third-party commitments, and a launch price uncoupled from anything 1996's PlayStation or Nintendo 64 were doing. **Roughly 42,000 units sold worldwide**, making it one of the worst-selling home consoles in history. Apple exited the format in 1997. The episode left Steve Jobs (returning later that same year) permanently allergic to cheap-derivative licensing — a quiet ancestor to the iPod and iPhone's tightly integrated single-vendor model.
