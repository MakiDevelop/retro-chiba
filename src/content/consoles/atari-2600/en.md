The actual foundation of the home-console industry — **128 bytes of RAM and 4 KB cartridges started the 8-bit era**. Released by Atari Inc. (then a Warner Communications subsidiary) in 1977, it took off the first year with the Space Invaders port. In 1979 a group of Atari programmers, frustrated by the lack of credit and royalties, walked out to found Activision — **inventing the third-party game-development company as an industry category**. By 1982, the Pac-Man port disaster, 5 million unsold E.T. cartridges, and a flood of low-quality third-party shovelware combined to trigger **the 1983 North American console crash**: industry revenue dropped 97% and the home console nearly vanished from the United States.
