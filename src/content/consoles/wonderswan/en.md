**The last handheld designed by Gunpei Yokoi** — the Game Boy creator left Nintendo, founded Koto, completed the WonderSwan prototype, and died in a car accident in 1997. Bandai picked up mass production and shipped it in 1999. The technical philosophy is unmistakably Yokoi's: **a single AA battery for 30 hours**, modest specs, strong content. The **portrait-or-landscape display**, switchable by physically rotating the unit, was a real innovation. **Japan-only** distribution; the Square Final Fantasy I/II/IV remakes did most of the sales lifting. Bandai exited the platform in 2003, ending the last serious Japanese challenge to Nintendo's handheld lock.
