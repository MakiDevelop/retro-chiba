**The first handheld with interchangeable cartridges** — a full decade ahead of the Game Boy. Milton Bradley (publisher of Connect Four and The Game of Life) made the cross-category attempt. The CPU lived in each cartridge, an unusual cost-cutting architecture. The **16×16 monochrome LCD — 256 pixels total, fewer than a modern emoji** — capped the ceiling at Pong, Breakout, and simple board games. By 1981, sunlight visibility issues and static-electricity LCD damage drove Milton Bradley out of the platform. **The Microvision is significantly under-credited in retro circles** — it is the foundational design statement of the swappable-cartridge handheld category.
