The standout of 1982's **second-wave high-spec home consoles** — Coleco moved up from its 1976 Telstar Pong experience to a proper 8-bit machine, and **the Donkey Kong pack-in** turned the box into "an arcade in your living room" overnight. Technically ahead of both the 2600 and Intellivision; the Expansion Module 1 made it the first home console with **official cross-platform backward compatibility** by accepting Atari 2600 cartridges. The 1983 crash took out the entire Wave 2 cohort — Coleco exited hardware in 1985, redirected to the Cabbage Patch Kids dolls business, and finally went bankrupt in 1988.
