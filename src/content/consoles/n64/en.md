Nintendo's cartridge holdout. **The first 64-bit home console**, the first mainstream analog thumbstick, and an SGI co-designed graphics processor that made 3D credible at home. But the refusal to abandon ROM cartridges drove Square out (and FF VII to the PlayStation), collapsing the third-party lineup; lifetime sales came in at roughly one-third of the PS1's. Engineering ahead, commerce behind — the textbook "right machine, wrong era."
