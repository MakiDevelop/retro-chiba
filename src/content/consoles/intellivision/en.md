Mattel's toys division mounted the most credible challenge to the Atari 2600 in the early 1980s — **"Intelligent Television"** was the marketing thesis, and the campaign hired George Plimpton to hold both machines on television and ask why anyone still owned the 2600. **It was the first directly comparative attack ad in the home-console industry.** Mattel's electronics group was wound down after the 1983 crash, but **INTV Corporation acquired the rights and kept the console in production until 1990** — outliving the Atari 5200 by six years.
