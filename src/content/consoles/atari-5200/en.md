Atari's attempt at the **computer-derived console** — repackaging the Atari 8-bit home-computer architecture into a games-only chassis. Several times more capable than the 2600. But the **analog joystick had no self-centering mechanism**: in Pac-Man the character kept drifting after the player let go of the stick. Combined with the 1983 crash that wiped out roughly 97% of the North American console market in two years, **the 5200 was discontinued in 1984 — just two years on the market**. It is Atari's fastest commercial failure and the company's most direct casualty of the crash.
