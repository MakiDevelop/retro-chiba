Nokia's attempt to invent a **phone-and-handheld hybrid** category, undermined by a cascade of design failures: portrait screen with landscape games, undersized keypad, game swaps that required pulling the battery. The **Sidetalking meme** — phone calls required holding the entire handheld sideways against the ear, because the microphone and earpiece were on the edges — became defining 2003–04 internet culture and the system's permanent reputation. The 2004 N-Gage QD revision corrected some issues but the market impression was fixed; Nokia exited the platform in 2005.
