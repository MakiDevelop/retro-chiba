Nintendo's first optical-disc home console (though using a proprietary mini-DVD rather than full DVD). Engineering was solid — the CPU outpaced the PS2's, overall performance was on par with Xbox — but the **capacity ceiling (1.5 GB vs the PS2's 8.5 GB)**, the **absence of a DVD-Video feature** that would have justified the box's place in a living room, and a thin third-party catalog kept it well behind the PS2 and roughly even with Xbox. The GameCube was a low point in Nintendo's home-console record, and the implicit lesson — "stop competing on raw specs" — became the foundation of the Wii.
